# FMEA Introduction

## Purpose
Introduce Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA) as a structured method to identify potential failures and their impact.

## FMEA Structure

| Item | Failure Mode | Effect | Cause | Severity | Occurrence | Detection | RPN |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Key Concepts

- Failure Mode: how something can fail
- Effect: consequence of the failure
- Cause: root cause
- Severity (S): impact of failure
- Occurrence (O): likelihood
- Detection (D): ability to detect before failure
- RPN = S × O × D

## Example

| Item | Failure Mode | Effect | Cause | S | O | D | RPN |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Battery | Power loss | Mission abort | Degradation | 9 | 3 | 4 | 108 |

## Engineering Value
- early failure identification
- prioritization of mitigation
- design improvement guidance
