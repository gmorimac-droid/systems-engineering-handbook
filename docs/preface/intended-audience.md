# Intended Audience

## Purpose

This page defines the primary audiences of the handbook and clarifies how different readers should approach the material. A systems engineering handbook becomes more useful when it is explicit about who it serves, what prior knowledge it assumes, and what kind of value each audience should expect to obtain from it.

## Primary Audiences

### Early-Career Systems Engineers

Readers entering the discipline often encounter fragmented definitions of systems engineering. For this audience, the handbook provides structure, terminology, and lifecycle context. It is particularly useful for understanding how stakeholder needs become requirements, how requirements influence architecture, and how architecture must ultimately be supported by integration and V&V evidence.

### Practicing Systems Engineers

For engineers already working in the discipline, the handbook serves as a reference framework. It can support the design of project documentation, technical review logic, artifact sets, and governance mechanisms. It is especially relevant for engineers seeking a more consistent way to connect method, deliverables, and lifecycle decision points.

### Technical Leads and Chief Engineers

Technical leaders often need a concise but rigorous way to communicate expectations to teams. This handbook can support discussions on baselines, technical authority, decision governance, configuration control, review readiness, and evidence quality.

### Multidisciplinary Team Members

Software engineers, hardware engineers, test engineers, safety engineers, product owners, and operations personnel may all interact with systems engineering outputs without being systems engineers themselves. For these readers, the handbook helps explain the role and rationale of artifacts such as ConOps, requirements baselines, interface definitions, risk registers, and verification matrices.

### Students and Researchers

The site can also be used as a structured learning scaffold. While it is not a formal academic textbook, it can support coursework, project-based learning, and self-study by showing how conceptual topics map to project artifacts and lifecycle activities.

## Secondary Audiences

The handbook may also be useful for:

- project managers seeking a clearer view of engineering maturity and review readiness
- quality and assurance personnel who need visibility into technical baselines and evidence structures
- documentation architects and knowledge managers building an engineering portal
- organizations establishing or refining an internal systems engineering playbook

## Assumed Background

The handbook assumes a general familiarity with engineering work and technical projects, but it does not assume deep prior knowledge of systems engineering. Some sections are introductory, while others are written with a more advanced tone appropriate for readers who already understand the lifecycle and artifact logic of complex systems.

## Limits of Audience Coverage

No single handbook can fully satisfy every possible role. Some readers will require more depth in regulatory compliance, formal modeling, safety certification, or domain-specific design practices than this project currently provides. The present objective is to support a broad but coherent systems engineering audience while leaving room for later specialization.
