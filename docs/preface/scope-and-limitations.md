# Scope and Limitations

## Scope

This handbook covers the systems engineering discipline at a professional and lifecycle-oriented level. It addresses how complex systems are defined, decomposed, analyzed, governed, integrated, verified, validated, operated, and sustained. The emphasis is on **coherence across engineering activities**, not on deep specialization in any single adjacent discipline.

The scope includes:

- conceptual foundations of systems engineering
- requirements engineering and traceability
- system architecture and decomposition
- model-based perspectives at an introductory level
- engineering analysis and trade studies
- integration planning and interface control
- verification, validation, and evidence logic
- risk and opportunity management
- configuration, baselines, and change control
- quality, reviews, and governance
- operations, support, and obsolescence
- artifacts and documentation structures
- application to the AIIDS case study

## Limitations

The handbook has important limits.

It does not provide domain-complete guidance for certification, airworthiness approval, regulatory compliance, contractual flow-down, or tool-specific implementation. It also does not attempt to replace formal standards or organizational procedures.

In addition, the case study is intentionally scoped to remain understandable and reusable. It therefore simplifies certain real-world concerns such as procurement constraints, manufacturing qualification detail, regulatory approval pathways, and detailed safety assurance cases.

## Intended Use Within These Limits

The content should be used as:

- a reference framework
- a structured teaching and learning baseline
- a documentation architecture for a project repository
- a professional example of systems-engineering-oriented knowledge organization

It should not be used uncritically as a complete substitute for project tailoring, formal standards interpretation, or contractual engineering obligations.
