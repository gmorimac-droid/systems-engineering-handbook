# Trade Studies

## Purpose

Trade studies are structured engineering analyses used to compare alternative solutions against defined criteria. Their purpose is to support rational, transparent, and traceable decision-making.

## Context

In Systems Engineering, trade studies are performed when multiple design options exist and a selection must be made based on performance, cost, risk, and other factors.

## Trade Study Process

A typical trade study follows these steps:

1. Define the problem
2. Identify alternatives
3. Define evaluation criteria
4. Assign weights to criteria
5. Evaluate each alternative
6. Compare results
7. Document the decision

## Evaluation Criteria

Common criteria include:

- Performance
- Cost
- Risk
- Complexity
- Scalability
- Maintainability

## Scoring Methods

Trade studies may use:

- qualitative comparison
- weighted scoring
- decision matrices
- simulation or modeling

## Example Matrix

| Criteria | Weight | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Performance | 0.4 | 8 | 7 | 9 |
| Cost | 0.3 | 6 | 9 | 5 |
| Risk | 0.3 | 7 | 6 | 8 |

## Engineering Implications

Trade studies:

- reduce subjective decisions
- support justification of design choices
- improve transparency
- enable repeatability
